How It Works

The 3-Coloring Problem

Can you color each vertex of a graph with one of three colors such that no two adjacent vertices share the same color? This is an NP-complete problem.

Zero-Knowledge Proof

The prover can convince the verifier that they know a valid 3-coloring without revealing the actual coloring. Here's how:

  1. Commitment: Hide all vertex colors using cryptographic commitments
  2. Challenge: Verifier picks a random edge to check
  3. Response: Reveal only the colors of the two vertices connected by that edge

By repeating this process many times, the verifier gains confidence that the prover knows a valid coloring, without ever seeing the complete solution.
